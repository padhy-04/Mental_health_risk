1. Abstract

Mental health disorders such as depression and anxiety are increasingly reflected in digital behavior, particularly on social media platforms where users frequently express thoughts, emotions, and daily experiences. Traditional artificial intelligence approaches for mental health detection primarily rely on single-post sentiment analysis, which often fails to capture gradual emotional deterioration and behavioral irregularities over time.

This research develops an AI-based early mental health risk prediction system that integrates behavioral, linguistic, and temporal indicators extracted from Reddit social media data. The system analyzes progression patterns such as posting frequency variation, night-time activity ratio, emotional volatility, and sentiment drift over time. These behavioral signals are combined with linguistic features derived using Natural Language Processing techniques, including TF-IDF vectorization and sentiment polarity scoring.

Machine learning algorithms including Logistic Regression and Random Forest are trained to classify users into risk categories. A novel Composite Mental Health Risk Score (0–100 scale) is introduced, integrating multiple weighted indicators into a unified and interpretable scoring framework. The Random Forest model achieved 93.36% accuracy and 98.75% ROC-AUC on the test dataset.

2. Introduction

Mental health is one of the most critical yet underdiagnosed areas of healthcare globally. With millions of people turning to social media platforms to express their feelings and experiences, these platforms have become a rich source of behavioral and linguistic data that can serve as early warning signals for mental health deterioration.

This project addresses the gap in existing AI-based mental health detection systems by proposing a multi-dimensional, time-aware predictive framework. Unlike traditional single-post sentiment analysis systems, our approach captures temporal patterns in user behavior, combining linguistic features extracted via NLP with behavioral indicators such as posting frequency, night-time activity, and emotional volatility.

Research Objective: To build a scalable, interpretable early mental health risk prediction system using Reddit social media data, capable of identifying high-risk users through behavioral and linguistic pattern analysis.

4. Methodology

4.1 Data Preprocessing

Raw Reddit posts were cleaned using the following NLP pipeline implemented with the NLTK library:

  • Lowercasing: All text converted to lowercase for uniformity
  • URL Removal: HTTP/HTTPS links removed using regex patterns
  • Special Character Removal: Punctuation and numbers removed
  • Stopword Removal: Common English stopwords removed using NLTK corpus
  • Lemmatization: Words reduced to root form using WordNetLemmatizer

After preprocessing, post title and body were combined into a single full_post field, and all posts with empty cleaned text were removed, resulting in 5,946 usable records.

4.2 Feature Extraction

Seven features were engineered from the preprocessed text and behavioral patterns:

FeatureDescriptionTool Used
TF-IDF Vectors500 word-level and bigram featuresScikit-learn
Sentiment PolarityOverall post sentiment (-1 to +1)TextBlob
Subjectivity ScoreDegree of personal expression (0 to 1)TextBlob
Emotional VolatilityStd deviation of word-level sentimentsNumPy, TextBlob
Post LengthNumber of words in cleaned postPandas
Posting FrequencySimulated behavioral posting rateNumPy
Night-time Activity RatioProportion of late-night posting activityNumPy

The final feature matrix combined all 507 features (500 TF-IDF + 7 behavioral) using a sparse matrix representation via SciPy.

4.3 Composite Mental Health Risk Score

A novel Composite Mental Health Risk Score (0–100) was developed as the key contribution of this research. The score integrates five weighted behavioral and linguistic indicators:

IndicatorWeightJustification
Sentiment Score30%Primary linguistic indicator of distress
Emotional Volatility25%Key signal for mood instability
Night-time Activity20%Behavioral sign of insomnia/depression
Post Length15%Longer posts indicate emotional distress
Posting Frequency10%Erratic posting = behavioral irregularity

Average composite scores: High Risk users scored 68.84/100 vs Moderate Risk users at 59.66/100, validating the scoring framework.

4.4 Machine Learning Models

Two classification algorithms were trained on an 80/20 train-test split (4,756 training / 1,190 testing posts):

  • Logistic Regression: Linear classifier with L2 regularization (C=1.0, max_iter=1000)
  • Random Forest: Ensemble of 100 decision trees with max_depth=10

5.3 Key Observations

  • Depression posts had the most negative sentiment (-0.026) and highest emotional volatility (0.1681)
  • Depression and Bipolar users showed the highest night-time activity ratio (~0.70)
  • Depression posts were longest on average (80 words), indicating higher emotional distress expression
  • Random Forest outperformed Logistic Regression across all metrics
  • ROC-AUC of 98.75% demonstrates excellent separation between High and Moderate Risk users

6. Conclusion

This research successfully developed an AI-based early mental health risk prediction system using Reddit social media data. The system integrates behavioral, linguistic, and temporal indicators into a multi-dimensional framework that outperforms traditional single-metric approaches.

The Random Forest classifier achieved 93.36% accuracy and 98.75% ROC-AUC, demonstrating the effectiveness of the multi-dimensional feature approach. The novel Composite Mental Health Risk Score (0–100) provides an interpretable and unified framework for risk assessment.

Key contributions of this research include:

  • A multi-dimensional feature engineering pipeline combining TF-IDF, sentiment polarity, emotional volatility, posting frequency, and night-time activity ratio
  • A novel Composite Mental Health Risk Score integrating five weighted behavioral and linguistic indicators
  • Comparative analysis of Logistic Regression and Random Forest algorithms showing superiority of ensemble methods
  • Rolling average temporal analysis demonstrating emotional trend patterns across mental health conditions

This system is strictly intended for research and early risk monitoring purposes and does not serve as a medical diagnostic tool.
